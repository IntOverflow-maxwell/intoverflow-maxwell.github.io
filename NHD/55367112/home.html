PREFACE
In all of humanities’ time on Earth, specific moments in time forever alter history. These are turning points, events in history that leave an indelible mark on our present and future. Caesar, Napoleon, Washington, and many more, famous or infamous, left behind myriad legacies to be told through generations. Yet there are many more figures left relatively unknown, whose legacies have defined our world - their stories untold.

How many people think of the birth of the Roman Empire?

How many people remember Gaius Marius?

INTRODUCTION
By the year 105 B.C.E, Rome was in a state of crisis. It had manpower shortage, inexperienced generals, and a corrupt and incompetent Senate was failing to address the aforementioned crisis properly. Half of the Roman army was killed with another half forced to shamefully surrender, a humiliating defeat in West Africa. This loss of Roman pride was further exacerbated by the destruction of two entire armies at Arausio by Germanic tribes, where 120,000 men were lost.
Rome: Ruins of the Forum, Looking towards the Capitol, Canaletto
This is the stage where our main protagonist of the story comes in: Gaius Marius. Born in a affluent Italian family in 157 B.C.E, Marius would eventually become consul seven times due to his popularity by the people. Using his power, he would implement a series of military reforms that would fundamentally change the history of Rome. While some of these changes may not have been invented by him, he was the first to implement them in a broad, effective and permanent fashion. This website dedicates itself to presenting the changes, their effect, and why they’re a major turning point in history.
